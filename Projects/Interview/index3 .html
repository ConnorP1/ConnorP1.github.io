This discussion covers everything from fascinations with geometry to daily routines. We hope you enjoy!
Lets jump right into this, starting off, where were you born/from?
I was born in NYC and lived in Brooklyn till I was around 4. Then we moved to Jersey and that's where I was pretty much raised. Now I live in Brooklyn again though.
Cool, what was it like growing up there? What things would you get into?
It was nice overall. New Jersey tends to carry a rather negative stigma to it when brought up in conversation. Some of it is warranted haha, but I think there's alot of cool aspects about the place. Where I was was still within arms reach of the city so naturally I was over there as much as possible, especially after I started skating. I'm super grateful that I kinda had a taste of both. Like, I love being around nature. Being around trees, hiking, doing yard work, stuff like that is really important to me. And then on the other side of that, I love being in the city. I've met some of the people I love most here. It truly can be such a special place. I think it's important to have a balance though. Sometimes you need that interaction, other times you need to be somewhere quiet and just be with yourself.
Definetly. Making an effort to balance both is so necessary. When and how did you start skateboarding? We're you always into it?
I was either 9 or 10 when I started. I had a budget 'Toys R Us' skateboard that had been in my garage for awhile and had brought it out during a family bbq. I remember talking to my friend who I walked home from school with about how cool it was and he said his older brother had one. We pretty much became best friends after that. We would just skate around the neighborhood and watch YouTube videos made by random kids skating in their driveways.
Do you think YouTube and the internet in general impacted the things you were into as a kid?
Oh absolutely. I was super into YouTube and computers as a whole as a kid. I actually played most games using a computer opposed to an Xbox. Looking back it was actually kinda cool.
Oh thats cool. What kinda games were you on?
Oh man so many things. I was definetly into random online websites like Miniclip, but then was definetly into Call of Duty. There were a lot of niche ones as well. I had a long period of time where I was really obsessed with old Mario games, and any old Nintendo or Atari stuff. There was something about the way stuff looked that made it so intriguing. My friend and I would find the original Mario games online and recode little things, or we'd find these rare extensions for the DS haha. It was cool though, all those things made me more aware. I think nerding out on all of those things was actually a good thing.
Yeah a lot of older video games have something fascinating about them thats hard to explain haha. What other things fascinate you?
Haha everything. I dont know, I guess anything I don't fully understand at face value fascinates me. I think math and science are actually so cool. I'm not wired like that either, but how geomemtry presents itself in nature endlessly amazes me. I think wood and metal working is really cool. Tapestry and intricate quilting art is so beautiful. I think dreams are fascinating and confusing. Light can be breathtaking and hard to describe. Old clothes. Computers. Trucks. I think the early to mid 2000s is a really interesting time. This could go on forever if I wanted it to.
What kind of truck would you want?
Ohhh, a late 90s/early 2000s Toyota Tacoma or a late 80s Ford F-250.
What does a normal morning look like for you? Lets say if you weren't in a rush to get somewhere or do something.
If I don't have to be somewhere its nice to not wake up to an alarm. Put on pants but no shirt. I always make coffee. I might play music or a podcast. Then when the coffee's ready I make a cup with some almond milk. Then I make a smoothie or some oatmeal. Lately I've been into writing things down so I'll keep something nearby to write in. Probably check my phone. Sometimes if I'm the only one there I'll pace around or dance a little.
What are 3 things you want to get better at?
Focusing on one thing at a time. Implementing a daily stretching routine. Surfing.
Alright. I think we'll end it there. Thanks for this conversation. I wish you all the best!
Likewise! Thank you, take care.
